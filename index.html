The practice you are visiting was built by Dr. James Marsh.

For decades, Dr. James Marsh was one of the names patients in Sarasota gave each other when a friend asked who to trust for plastic surgery. His practice on South Tamiami Trail was a quiet anchor in the city's medical community.

When Dr. Marsh chose his successor, the decision was not made through a brokerage listing or a private-equity acquisition. It was made by a surgeon evaluating who he would trust with the patients he had spent his career caring for. That surgeon was Brandon Lambiris.

"The practice address has not changed. The standard of care it was built on has not changed. The job is to carry that forward, with the techniques and training a generation of plastic surgery research has added since."

Dr. Lambiris is board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery (ABPS), the recognized board for plastic surgery in the United States. This certification is distinct from cosmetic-only boards that some Florida practitioners hold.

Born and trained in Florida, he completed his undergraduate degree at Florida State University and his medical degree at FSU College of Medicine, where he was inducted into Alpha Omega Alpha, the national medical honor society reserved for the top of each class. He was selected as only the second medical student in the history of FSU's College of Medicine to be awarded an Integrated Plastic Surgery residency, a six-year program at the University of Missouri-Columbia where he served as Chief Resident in his final year.

Who took over Dr. James Marsh's plastic surgery practice in Sarasota?

The practice is now led by Dr. Brandon Lambiris, MD, who is board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery (ABPS). The practice operates at the same Sarasota address Dr. Marsh built his career at, 2677 S Tamiami Trail, Suite 3.

Is Dr. Brandon Lambiris board-certified?

Yes. Dr. Lambiris is board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery (ABPS), the board recognized by the American Board of Medical Specialties for plastic surgery in the United States. He is also a member of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, The Aesthetic Society, and the Florida Society of Plastic Surgeons.

Where did Dr. Lambiris train?

Dr. Lambiris completed his undergraduate degree at Florida State University, his medical degree at Florida State University College of Medicine (inducted into Alpha Omega Alpha, the national medical honor society), and a six-year Integrated Plastic Surgery residency at the University of Missouri-Columbia, where he served as Chief Resident in his final year. He was the second medical student in the history of FSU's College of Medicine to be awarded an Integrated Plastic Surgery residency.

What procedures does Dr. Lambiris perform?

Dr. Lambiris performs the full range of plastic surgery procedures including breast augmentation, breast lift, breast reduction, mommy makeover, tummy tuck, liposuction, BBL, facelift, blepharoplasty, rhinoplasty, and post-weight-loss body contouring. The practice also operates an in-house medical spa offering injectables, laser treatments, and skincare.

Does Dr. Lambiris perform injectables himself, or does a nurse handle them?

Dr. Lambiris performs injectables himself in this practice. This is a deliberate structural choice, not an industry default. When you book follow-up injectables, the same surgeon you booked your procedure with performs them.

What financing options are available?

The practice works with CareCredit, PatientFi, Cherry, and Alphaeon Credit. Most patients build a payment plan with one of these partners. Plans typically range from 12 to 60 months depending on procedure cost and credit profile. Approvals are usually returned the same day. Estimated payment ranges are available on each procedure page.
